Privacy Policy

Battery Charging Monitor — com.honestsimpleapps.batterymonitor
Effective date: March 19, 2026

Zero permissions. Zero tracking. Zero background drain.

Overview

Battery Charging Monitor is a utility app that reads battery information from your device's Android BatteryManager API. This privacy policy explains what data the app accesses, what it stores, and how advertising works.

Permissions

Battery Charging Monitor requires zero runtime permissions. The BatteryManager API provides battery data (percentage, temperature, voltage, current, health, charge status) without any permission. The app does not request access to your camera, location, contacts, storage, microphone, or any other device capability.

The only permission present in the app manifest is INTERNET, which is a "normal" Android permission (not a "dangerous" permission) and is required solely for loading advertisements via Google AdMob. If you purchase "Remove Ads," no network requests are made by the app.

We do not collect any personal data.

Data Stored on Your Device

The app stores a small amount of data locally on your device using Android DataStore (Preferences). This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third party.

  • Theme preference: Light, Dark, or System
  • Temperature unit: Celsius or Fahrenheit
  • Ad removal status: Whether you purchased the "Remove Ads" in-app purchase
  • Ad-free timer: Timestamp for when a rewarded ad-free period expires
  • Charging sessions: Session history (start/end percentage, duration, charge type, average current) stored as serialized data. Maximum 50 sessions retained.

Background Work

Battery Charging Monitor runs zero background services. No WorkManager jobs, no alarms, no wake locks. The app only reads battery data while it is in the foreground and visible. It does zero work when not on screen. The app itself cannot drain your battery because it has no background execution at all.

Advertising

The free version of Battery Charging Monitor displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob. This includes banner ads and timed interstitial ads. The AdMob SDK may collect device identifiers (such as the Android Advertising ID) for the purpose of serving and personalizing advertisements.

You can control ad personalization through your Android device settings: Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization.

The app also offers optional rewarded ads: you may choose to watch a short video advertisement to temporarily go ad-free for 20 minutes, or complete a brief survey to go ad-free for 40 minutes. These are entirely voluntary and user-initiated.

For details on how Google handles data collected through AdMob, please refer to Google's Privacy Policy.

In-App Purchases

Battery Charging Monitor offers a single in-app purchase: "Remove Ads" for $0.99. This is a one-time, non-consumable purchase processed through Google Play Billing. After purchase, no ads are displayed and no ad-related network requests are made. The purchase restores automatically across reinstalls via your Google Play account.

We do not process payments directly. All payment processing is handled by Google Play. See Google Payments Privacy Notice.

Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app contains advertisements which may not be suitable for or directed at children.

Third-Party Services

The app uses the following third-party services:

  • Google AdMob — for displaying advertisements. Privacy Policy
  • Google Play Billing — for processing in-app purchases. Privacy Notice

Data Security

All network communication (ad requests, billing verification) is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS. Local preferences and charging session data are stored in Android's app-private data directory, accessible only to the app itself.

Your Rights

Since we do not collect or store any personal data on our servers, there is no personal data to access, export, or delete. Local preferences and charging session history can be cleared by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's data through Android Settings.
